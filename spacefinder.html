Study space finder.

Problem space.

University of Toronto Mississauga (UTM) offers many spaces for students to study, along with several facilities to aid students' studies. However, the study spaces are often packed with students, as there are more students than study spaces in UTM. To make matters worse, there are no services that offer real-time updates of the capacity of study spaces - it is very difficult to find unoccupied spots in the UTM study spaces.

Questionnaire.

We wanted to gather quantitative and qualitative data about how UTM students feel about the availability of study spaces. We designed a questionnaire with 11 multiple-choice questions using Google Forms. The questionnaires were deliverable to students both through social media and through in-person encounters and ended up receiving 67 valid responses.

The above is a part of results from our questionnaire.

  • 79.1% of the students feel that their preferred type of studyspace is often crowded.
  • The silent and quiet zones are overcrowding.
  • Among the students who prefer not to study on campus, 61.5% of them indicated that it was because they could not focus.
  • 79.1% of our respondents have encountered problem when using the UTM study space and only 20.9% said there was no problem.
  • 46.2% of students find the overcrowding of study spaces a problem.

Interview.

Conducting in-person interviews would provide us with a perspective and voice that would go beyond answering multiple choice questions. As a result, we conducted 2 interviews with 11 questions with UTM students.

Main Insights:
  • Each study space on campus serves a different role and holistically plays an important role in deciding which study space is best for a specific student and their needs.
  • There is significant variability in the amount of time spent studying at a specific study spot.
  • Not only do students need computers on campus, but some may also need specific hardware and software requirements. Our solution should be able to address this problem and differentiate between similar study spaces that satisfy different underlying requirements.

Low-fidelity prototype & evaluation.

Study Format: In-person

Evaluation Method: Cognitive walkthroughs

  • Confusion of the product features.
  • Misleading symbols and other presentations.
  • Too complicated operating system.
  • UI and cosmetic issues.

What added.

  • A "quick guide" when the first time using the app.
  • Use more well-known representations/icons, e.g., numbers, to express.
  • Suggested or popular locations around the search bar to hint the users.
  • Small scroll and swipe icons on some of the interfaces.
  • A main finding summary on the detail page to show the users of their desired places.

High-fidelity prototype.

In the prototype of UTM Study Space Finder, you will find the following functions:

  • List of all UTM buildings which have study space (7 in total), each can be clicked to check more details.
  • Locate the user on campus to find the nearest building (sort by distance) and the most available building (sort by availability).
